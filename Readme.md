# 📊 EDA – Student Dataset

## 1. Project Overview

This project focuses on performing Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a student dataset to understand digital behavior patterns and their potential impact on mental well-being.

The analysis covers:

- Gender-wise platform usage comparison  
- Age-wise screen time trends  
- Academic level-wise digital behavior  
- Platform-wise addiction score analysis  
- Relationship between daily screen time and mental health score  
- Impact of sleep duration on mental well-being  

The objective is to identify patterns in digital consumption and highlight potential risk factors affecting student mental health.

## 5. Key Insights

- Platform usage varies across gender and academic levels.
- Younger students show relatively higher daily screen time.
- Higher screen time is linked with increased addiction scores.
- Students with longer sleep duration tend to report better mental health scores.
- Certain platforms demonstrate higher average addiction levels.


## 6. Recommendations

- Promote digital well-being awareness programs in schools and colleges.
- Encourage balanced screen time and healthy sleep habits.
- Identify high-risk student groups for early intervention.
- Conduct further statistical analysis (correlation/regression) to validate findings.
